Course Overview

About This Course

This course provides a comprehensive introduction to macroeconomic theory and policy analysis using New Keynesian frameworks. Students will develop analytical skills to understand economic fluctuations, inflation dynamics, and the role of monetary and fiscal policy in stabilizing the economy.

Learning Objectives

By the end of this course, students will be able to:

  • Analyze short-run and long-run economic fluctuations using IS-LM-PC models
  • Evaluate the effectiveness of monetary and fiscal policy interventions
  • Understand the microfoundations of New Keynesian macroeconomic theory
  • Apply macroeconomic models to real-world economic policy debates
  • Critically assess macroeconomic data and forecasting methods

Prerequisites

ECON 204 (Principles of Macroeconomics) and MATH 155 (Calculus) or equivalent.

Course Policies

Attendance Policy

Regular attendance is expected and contributes to your participation grade. If you must miss class due to illness or emergency, please email me in advance when possible. Make-up quizzes will only be offered for documented absences.

Late Work Policy

Problem sets submitted late will receive a 10% deduction per day late. Assignments more than one week late will not be accepted unless prior arrangements have been made. The lowest problem set score will be dropped to account for unexpected circumstances.

Academic Integrity

Collaboration on problem sets is encouraged, but each student must submit their own work. Copying solutions from other students or online sources is not permitted. All exam work must be completed independently.

Accommodations

Students who require accommodations should contact Student Disability Services and provide documentation as early as possible in the semester. I will work with you to ensure you have equal access to course materials and assessments.

Communication

The best way to reach me is via email. I typically respond within 24 hours during weekdays. For urgent matters or complex questions, please come to office hours or schedule an appointment.

Grade Disputes

If you have concerns about a grade, please wait 24 hours after receiving feedback, then schedule a meeting during office hours. Come prepared to explain your reasoning in writing.
